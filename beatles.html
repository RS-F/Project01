The Beatles in Hamburg

Introduction

"The Beatles" is known as one of the most popular rockband ever. Founded in 1957 as a group of young students ("The Quarrymen") they became famous worldwide. John Lennon (*1940 +1980), Paul Mccartney (*1942), George Harrison (*1943 +2001) and Ringo Starr (real name: Richard Starkey / *1940) changed the history of popular music fundamentaly. Hamburg is an important milestone in Beatles history because here they learned how to play as a group.

Grosse Freiheit

At "Große Freiheit" - a part of the red light district next to the "Reeperbahn"- several music clubs took place the Beatles also had performed, eg Kaiserkeller, The Indra and the Top Ten. In the latter Singer Tony Sheridan recorded with the Beatles (for this session the band was called "The Beat Brothers") „My Bonnie“, known as the groups first record ever.

Beatles Square

The Beatles Square (at Reeperbahn / Große Freiheit) was established 2008 showing five statues. The Square is round designed with a black surface similar to a record.

Star Club

"In 1962 Horst Fasher and Manfred Weissleder opened the Star Club at first time. Young people listened to the music of Chuck Berre, Bill Haley, Jimi Hendrix and especially the Beatles. In 1962 was the last performance at Star Club while in 1969 the club history ended.

Performance 1960

Picture shows John, George, Pete Best, Paul and Stuart Sutcliffe. Being in the age between Teen and Twen the made their experiences living at Reeperbahn. Stuart met Astrid Kirchherr and left group in 1960.

Bravo Beatles Blitz Tour 1966

After becoming popular the first (and also the last) tour in Germany was supported by famous german youth-magazin „Bravo“. Beyond Hamburg (Ernst-Merck-Halle, two concerts) four more performances (two for each location) were given in in Essen ( Grughalle) and Munich (Zirkus Krone). Later Bundeskanzler Helmut Schmidt and wife were seen among the audience.

Instruments 1966

Here you can see the instruments Beatles played in Germany 1966 at Bravo-Blitz-Tour.

Beatles At Panoptikum

Panoptikum is a wax-museum (like Madame Toussauds in London). You can see the figures of the fab four, also of Elvis Presley and Marylin Monroe.
